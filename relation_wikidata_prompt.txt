Role: You are a Supply Chain Ontology Engineer and Knowledge Graph Architect.

Objective: Generate a curated list of Wikidata properties to construct a Knowledge Graph that covers the full depth of a modern digital supply chain, grounded in the SCOR-DS and Enterprise Ontology frameworks.

Context (Guardrails):
Use the following frameworks as your strict validation criteria. Every property you select must map directly to one of these specific concepts or phases.

1. SCOR-DS (Digital Standard) Processes:

Orchestrate: Governance, business rules, ESG, compliance, and structural hierarchy (e.g., regulatory bodies, parent/child structures).
Plan: Synchronizing supply/demand (e.g., capacity planning, resource allocation).
Order: Order lifecycle management (e.g., contracts, purchase requirements, client relationships).
Source: Procurement of goods/services (e.g., raw materials, energy inputs, supplier selection).
Transform: Converting inputs into finished products (e.g., manufacturing, chemical processing, assembly, branding).
Fulfill: Logistics and flow to customer (e.g., distribution centers, shipping methods, physical locations).
Return: Reverse flow (e.g., waste management, recycling, repairs).

2. Enterprise Ontology (Business Concepts):

Activity: Plan, Resource, Doer, Authority, Capability.
Organisation: Corporation, Partnership, Legal Entity, Ownership, Stakeholder, Employment Contract.
Strategy: Goal, Objective, Mission, Risk, Decision.
Marketing: Vendor, Customer, Reseller, Product, Market Segment, Competitor, Brand Image.
Time: Time Interval, Time Point.
Task:
Identify valid Wikidata properties (wdt:P...) that serve as the edges in a supply chain knowledge graph.

Critical Requirements:

1. Enforce Heterogeneity: You must look beyond "Organization-to-Organization" links. You are required to find properties that connect Organizations to:
	Products (e.g., what they make).
	Materials (e.g., what they use).
	Locations (e.g., where they operate).
	Standards/Regulations (e.g., what they comply with).
	Waste/Recycling (e.g., what they recycle).
	
2. Avoid Generic Noise: Exclude generic properties like "instance of" (P31) or "country" (P17) unless they specifically denote a supply chain function (e.g., "country of origin" for a product).

Selection Criteria:

1. Relevance: The property must concretely map to a specific SCOR-DS phase or Enterprise Ontology concept.
2. Validity: Must be a real, functioning Wikidata property.
3. Generalizability: Must be applicable across industries (tech, automotive, retail).

Output Format: 
	(Strictly one per line):wdt:[P-Code] ([relation label])
	
Example Output:
wdt:P176 (manufacturer)
wdt:P186 (material used)
wdt:P1056 (product or material produced)




##### NOTES #######
- The prompt was used in multiple openly available chat models in January 2026, GPT-5.0, Deepseek V-3.2, Gemini Flash 2.5 
- Relations that were completely irrelevant were removed based on manual interpretation. And those that were unique in a certain model's response were added to the master list. 
- All labels were verified in wikidata to verify if the PIDs were valid or not.
